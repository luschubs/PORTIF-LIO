The Problem

Compliance is the law. Action is the design challenge.

MindLink helps organizations comply with Brazil's NR-01 regulation — mandatory psychosocial risk mapping, enforcement beginning in 2026.

Their diagnostic process generates massive data — survey responses, risk classifications, participation rates, severity levels — all delivered as static PDF reports. HR teams had no way to explore data dynamically, compare departments, or track progress over time.

The challenge: transform document-based delivery into a living, actionable dashboard.

Design Process

From zero brand to functional prototype.

01

Brand Identity

Built MindLink's complete identity from zero — the dashed infinity logo, deep teal palette, and typography for clinical credibility.

02

Information Architecture

Mapped 5 core screens around what HR actually asks: Overview, Departments, Detail, Action Plan (kanban), and Reports.

03

UI + Vibe Coding

Light theme with dark teal sidebar in Figma, then translated to a fully interactive React build via AI-assisted development.

Key Decisions

Three choices that shaped the product.

A

Action Plan as first-class screen

Most dashboards stop at showing data. MindLink connects insights to actions — a kanban tracking interventions from Pending to Completed.

B

Severity as color, not just numbers

HR professionals aren't data analysts. Every score paired with a traffic-light indicator. Scannable in seconds.

C

AI as a design tool

Used Claude to bridge static mockups and real interaction. Design decisions stayed mine — AI compressed the build cycle.
