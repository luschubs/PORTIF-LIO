Premium identity for an ophthalmology hospital.

A high-contrast design system that balances refinement with visual accessibility — for an audience that trusts the brand with their sight.

Design System

The Problem

Premium aesthetic. Clinical credibility. Both, at once.

Mondevi is an ophthalmology hospital that needed an institutional website transmitting sophistication and medical confidence simultaneously. The audience: patients trusting the clinic with their sight.

The constraint that defined everything: premium aesthetic had to coexist with high accessibility. An ophthalmology hospital that ignores contrast would be a brand failure.

Approach

High-contrast as a design principle, not an afterthought.

Most healthcare brands lean either clinical (cold, sterile) or warm (generic, friendly). Mondevi needed a third path: editorial elegance with maximum legibility.

I built the design system around two non-negotiables: WCAG AA contrast ratios on every text-background pair and a typography hierarchy that scales from desktop to mobile without losing weight.

01

Editorial typography

Serif display + sans body. Restrained, magazine-like — far from typical clinic stock photography aesthetics.

02

Earth-tone palette

Deep browns and creams instead of the medical blues. Warmer, premium — and ironically more accessible than the clinical defaults.

03

Generous whitespace

Confident negative space. Information presented in clear hierarchies — never cluttered, always scannable.

Outcome

A site that trusts its audience.

The final design was implemented by a development partner in HTML/CSS, with the design system documented for future iterations. The site now serves as Mondevi's digital flagship — the first impression for patients researching specialized eye care.

What I'd carry forward: premium and accessible aren't opposites. The constraint of accessibility actually made the design more refined, not less.
